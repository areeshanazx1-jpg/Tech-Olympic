Safety Guidelines

Comprehensive safety guidelines and emergency response procedures to help you stay prepared and safe.

Emergency Response Guidelines

Medical Emergencies

1
Call Emergency Services

Immediately call 102 for ambulance. Provide your location, nature of injury, and number of people affected.

2
Keep Patient Calm & Still

Avoid moving the patient unless in immediate danger. Movement can worsen injuries, especially spinal injuries.

3
Monitor Vital Signs

Check consciousness, breathing, and pulse. Be prepared to perform CPR if needed until help arrives.

4
Provide Information

When paramedics arrive, inform them about symptoms, allergies, medications, and medical conditions.

Fire & Safety Emergencies

1
Evacuate Immediately

Leave the area as quickly as possible. Use stairs, never elevators. Close doors behind you to slow fire spread.

2
Alert Others

Warn people in the vicinity. Use fire alarms, sirens, or shout loudly to inform everyone of the danger.

3
Call Fire Brigade

Call 101 from a safe location. Provide exact address, type of emergency, and any people in danger.

4
Crawl Under Smoke

Smoke rises. Crawl on hands and knees where air is clearer. Cover nose and mouth with cloth.

Crime & Security Emergencies

1
Contact Police

Call 100 immediately. Report location, nature of crime, and suspect descriptions if safe to do so.

2
Prioritize Safety

Your safety is the priority. Move to a public place if possible. Don't confront criminals.

3
Provide Details

Share all information with police: suspect descriptions, vehicle details, weapons, and victims involved.

4
Don't Interfere

Wait for authorities. Interference can escalate situations and put you in danger.

Mental Health & Support

1
Reach Out for Help

If struggling, don't hesitate to contact mental health helplines and support services.

2
Listen Without Judgment

If someone confides in you, listen carefully and encourage them to seek professional help.

3
Seek Professional Help

Mental health is important. Consult psychiatrists, therapists, or counselors for support.

4
Suicide Prevention Hotline

Call 9152987821 for confidential support. Trained counselors are available 24/7 to help.

Important Safety Tips

Know Your Location

Always be aware of your address and nearby landmarks. This helps emergency services locate you quickly.

Save Emergency Numbers

Keep emergency contact numbers readily available in your phone and write them down.

Learn Basic First Aid

Take a first aid course. CPR and basic wound care can save lives in emergencies.

Stay Calm Under Pressure

Take deep breaths and remain focused. Panic can lead to poor decisions and increased danger.

Create an Emergency Plan

Prepare with family or friends. Identify safe locations and communication methods in advance.

Regular Safety Checks

Periodically review your safety measures and update emergency contact information.
